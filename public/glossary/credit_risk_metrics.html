Net Present Value (NPV) – current spot value of a transaction.

Current Exposure (CE) – only the positive portion of NPVs across a given set of transactions, reflecting the amount by which the counterparty may owe you if trades were immediately cancelled.

Effective Expected Positive Exposure (EEPE) – the time-weighted average of the running maximum EPE over the first year of the transaction.

Total Exposure – CE plus EEPE, a combination of actual (CE) and simulated future exposure (EEPE)

Expected Positive Exposure (EPE) – at a given point in time of simulated exposure, the average of all positive values across the NPV distribution.

Expected Negative Exposure (ENE) – at a given point in time of simulated exposure, the average of all negative values across the NPV distribution.

Potential Future Exposure (PFE) – at a given point in time of simulated exposure, a user-defined positive quantile across the NPV distribution.
