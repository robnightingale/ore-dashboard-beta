Funding Cost Adjustment (FCA) – the expected funding cost over the life of an uncollateralized derivative transaction, reflecting the asymmetry in funding costs and the common rate specified in the collateral agreement of a (hypothetical) hedging transaction.

Funding Benefit Adjustment (FBA) – the expected funding benefit over the life of an uncollateralized derivative transaction, reflecting the asymmetry in funding costs and the common rate specified in the collateral agreement of a (hypothetical) hedging transaction.

Funding Value Adjustment (FVA) – the net cost/benefit of FCA and FBA.

Collateral Valuation Adjustment (ColVA) – also known as OIS, the expected funding cost of variation margin (VM) over the life of a collateralized derivative transaction, reflecting the asymmetry in funding costs and the common rate specified in the collateral agreement.

Initial Margin (IM) – the amount of collateral required to be posted at a given point in time according to the Standard Initial Margin Method (SIMM), sufficient to cover 10-day VaR at 99% confidence.
